About This Market
Neighborhood-serving seasonal market in SOMA West
▼
SOMA West Farmers Market runs on Saturdays from July through November — a compact seasonal market serving the SoMa West corridor between the Mission and Civic Center. The July opening aligns with peak California summer: stone fruit, dry-farmed tomatoes, peppers, and corn are all at their best when this market opens. The November close captures the fall transition — squash, apples, citrus, and pomegranates. Because the season is short and late-summer focused, the produce quality during market months is consistently strong.

ABOUT THIS MARKET

SOMA West Farmers Market is a seasonal Saturday market at 398 12th Street, serving the SoMa West neighborhood — a residential pocket bounded by the Mission, Hayes Valley, and Civic Center. The market runs from July through November, overlapping with the peak of California's agricultural calendar. It provides fresh local produce access to a dense urban neighborhood that has relatively few alternative fresh food options within walking distance.
