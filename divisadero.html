FEATURED VENDORS · SUNDAY MARKET
Achadinha Cheese Company
Farmstead goat cheese · Petaluma, CA
▼
Jim and Donna Pacheco operate Achadinha on a family farm in Petaluma, making farmstead cheese from milk produced by their own goat herd. Farmstead means the animals and the cheesemaking are on the same property — the milk never travels. Their cheeses are fresh and aged, with varieties that change by season as the goats' diet shifts. The fresh chèvre is mild and creamy; the aged Capricious is sharper, crumblier, more complex. Achadinha is one of the most praised cheese vendors at any SF market and a reliable highlight at Divisadero.

Seasonal Produce & Stone Fruit
Berries, stone fruit, vegetables · Central Coast & Valley
▼
Divisadero carries a rotating selection of produce farms covering Bay Area weekly essentials — berries, stone fruit in summer, vegetables year-round. The market is praised for its community feel and its Lower Haight / Panhandle location, which serves a dense residential corridor without easy access to a larger market. Several farms rotate seasonally; the consistent weekday shopping options at nearby Civic Center make Divisadero more of a neighborhood supplement than a destination market.

ABOUT THIS MARKET

The Divisadero Farmers Market runs every Sunday year-round in the DMV parking lot on Fell Street at Broderick — between Fell and Oak, one block from the Panhandle. Its compact size and community orientation make it a practical weekly-shop option for residents of the Lower Haight, Western Addition, and Divisadero corridor. Neighbors in this stretch of the city make a direct line for the lot on Sunday mornings for fresh produce, sustainable meats, eggs, cheese, and prepared foods.
