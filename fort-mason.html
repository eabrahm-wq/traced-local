FEATURED VENDORS · SUNDAY MARKET
Resendiz Brothers Protea
Specialty cherries & exotic fruit · Fallbrook, CA
▼
The Resendiz family grows specialty cherries and exotic fruits in Fallbrook, San Diego County — an inland valley with the ideal microclimate for unusual fruit cultivation. Their cherries are a Fort Mason highlight: multiple varieties sold at peak ripeness, including late-season types that extend the cherry window beyond what most farms can offer. They also grow and sell varieties that rarely appear at Northern California markets, making their stall a reliable discovery stop for serious fruit shoppers.

Hmong Farm (Asian Greens)
Chinese broccoli, Asian greens & specialty vegetables · Central Valley
▼
Fort Mason is specifically called out by SF Standard food writers for its Hmong farm vendor selling Chinese broccoli (gai lan) and other Asian greens that are difficult to source in fresh, farm-direct form at Marina-area markets. Hmong agricultural communities in the Central Valley have been growing Southeast and East Asian specialty vegetables for Bay Area markets for decades. The farm brings gai lan, bok choy, water spinach, bitter melon, and other crops that rarely appear at farmer-facing markets north of downtown.
WHAT THEY BRING
Chinese broccoli (gai lan) Bok choy Yu choy Water spinach (on yong) ☀ Bitter melon (summer) Long beans
Region: Central Valley, CA
Community: Hmong farming tradition

Triple Delight Blueberries
Specialty blueberries & jam · Placer County, CA
▼
Triple Delight grows multiple blueberry varieties in Placer County, extending the season by staggering variety plantings. Their blueberry jam — frequently cited by Fort Mason regulars — is made from same-farm fruit with no artificial pectin. A summer seasonal anchor for the Fort Mason market's north SF shoppers.

ABOUT THIS MARKET

Fort Mason Farmers Market is hosted by the Golden Gate National Park Service at Fort Mason Center — the former U.S. Army West Coast Headquarters on the Marina waterfront, with direct views of the Golden Gate Bridge and Alcatraz. The market runs every Sunday year-round with 35+ vendors covering farm produce, sustainable meat and seafood, artisan breads, cut flowers, and prepared foods.

The setting gives Fort Mason a character distinct from any other SF market — the combination of waterfront access, Golden Gate views, and proximity to the Marina Green makes Sunday morning here a full experience, not just a shopping trip. Parking is free on-site, and the market is accessible by transit via the 28 and 30 Muni lines. Farmer spotlights and seasonal recipes are featured on the market website.
