Fifth Crow Farm
Heirloom vegetables & heritage beans · Pescadero, CA
CERTIFIED ORGANIC ▼
Fifth Crow Farm grows certified organic vegetables and heritage dry beans on the San Mateo coast in Pescadero. Their dry bean program is a standout — they cultivate locally adapted varieties including King City Pinks, a bean developed for Northern California conditions with a creamy texture and complex flavor that most commercial beans lack. The farm was founded by young farmers committed to building long-term soil health through cover cropping and composting. Their display at Clement Street is known for its visual range of richly hued vegetables.

King City Pink beans (dried) Heritage dry beans Specialty greens Root vegetables ☀ Heirloom tomatoes (Aug–Oct) ☀ Summer squash (Jun–Sep) 🍂 Winter squash (Sep–Jan)
Location: Pescadero, San Mateo County
Certifications: Certified Organic
Web: fifthcrowfarm.com

Daymoon Bread
Natural-leavened sourdough · SF-based · Clement-exclusive
▼
Daymoon Bread is one of three bakeries at Clement Street that cannot be found at any other SF farmers market — a distinction AIM highlights as a key differentiator of the Clement market. Daymoon makes small-batch natural-leavened sourdoughs using stone-milled flour, with long cold ferments that develop acidity and complexity. The breads are made in SF and baked the night before market day. Their loaves sell out consistently, making early arrival important for regulars who prioritize the bakery.
WHAT THEY BRING
Country sourdough Whole wheat loaves Rye blends Seasonal specialty loaves
Exclusivity: Clement Street Farmers Market only — not at any other SF market
Note: Sells out — arrive early

Marla Bakery
European-style pastry & sourdough · SF-based · Clement-exclusive
▼
Marla Bakery is a beloved SF bakery with a café in the Outer Richmond and a Clement Street market stall that's exclusive to this market. Their baking draws on European traditions — croissants made with premium French butter, brioche, tarts, and seasonal pastries — alongside hearty sourdough loaves. Marla is one of the SF bakeries that set the bar for what a neighborhood bakery can be, and their Clement presence is a direct extension of that neighborhood connection.
WHAT THEY BRING
Croissants & pain au chocolat Country sourdough Seasonal tarts Brioche Cookies
Exclusivity: Clement Street market only — not at other SF markets
Café: Outer Richmond, San Francisco

H&H Fresh Fish
Direct-sourced fresh seafood · Bay Area
▼
H&H Fresh Fish is a Bay Area seafood vendor with a consistent presence at Clement Street and several AIM markets. They source from Bay Area fishing operations and bring a rotating selection based on what's running. The Richmond District has one of SF's highest concentrations of Chinese and Southeast Asian cooking households, which makes a well-stocked seafood stall one of the market's most-used vendor slots.

ABOUT THIS MARKET

Clement Street Farmers Market is operated by the Agricultural Institute of Marin (AIM), a nonprofit farmers market operator founded in 1983 that runs nine certified markets across the Bay Area and supports over 400 farmers, food producers, and artisans from 40 California counties. At Clement, AIM has built one of the largest neighborhood markets in SF — 60+ vendors spanning three city blocks of the Inner Richmond's main commercial corridor.

The market's location in the Inner Richmond — a neighborhood with significant Chinese, Russian, and Southeast Asian communities — shapes its vendor mix: diverse produce including Asian vegetables and specialty crops alongside a strong bakery lineup that includes three bakeries (Daymoon, Marla, and Saltwater) that are exclusive to this market. AIM accepts CalFresh EBT, Market Match, and WIC. A Bounty Box curbside pickup program is available for shoppers who want pre-selected produce.
