Butchers

Independent butchers who buy whole animals directly from local ranchers. They break them down themselves, tell you what ranch the beef came from, and know the difference between a hanging tender and a hanger steak.

Not a grocery counter. A butcher.

WHY IT MATTERS

Most meat sold in the US passes through four companies. It is slaughtered in industrial facilities, portioned into vacuum-sealed primals, and shipped to grocery distribution centers. The animal has no name. The ranch has no address. The grocery store employee who slices it didn't choose it.

A real butcher bought a whole animal from a named ranch, hung it for three weeks, broke it down by hand, and can tell you exactly what cut is in front of you and why it matters.

SF BUTCHERS · PROFILED
Drewes Bros. Meats
California's oldest operating butcher · Est. 1889 · Noe Valley
EST. 1889 ▼
Drewes Brothers has been operating continuously on Church Street in Noe Valley since 1889 — through the 1906 earthquake, Prohibition, the Depression, two world wars, and the complete transformation of the American food system around them. It is California's oldest operating butcher shop. The current operation sources from Northern California ranches, ages beef in-house, and maintains a full custom-cut service. Drewes also appears at the Noe Valley Farmers Market three blocks away, bringing weekend cuts to the neighborhood market.
WHAT THEY CARRY
Dry-aged beef Pastured pork Lamb Housemade sausages Custom cuts Charcuterie Stocks & bones
Address: 1706 Church St, Noe Valley
Hours: Mon–Sat 9 AM–6 PM, Sun 10 AM–5 PM
Also at: Noe Valley Farmers Market (Sat)
4505 Meats
Whole-animal butchery & chicharrones · Mission / Ferry Plaza
LOCAL SOURCING ▼
Ryan Farr founded 4505 Meats out of a commitment to whole-animal butchery and direct farm relationships. The operation is best known for its chicharrones — made from heritage-breed pork — which have become a cult product at Ferry Plaza's Saturday market. The Mission retail shop offers full butchery service with named-ranch sourcing and staff who understand the cuts. 4505 is one of the few SF butchers that has built a brand around transparency: the animal's farm is always disclosed.
WHAT THEY CARRY
Heritage-breed pork Grass-fed beef Chicharrones Housemade hot dogs Charcuterie Sausage
Address: 705 Mariposa St, Mission
Also at: Ferry Plaza Farmers Market (Sat)
Avedano's Holly Park Market
Neighborhood butcher & deli · Bernal Heights

Avedano's occupies a historic butcher shop space in Bernal Heights and operates as both a full-service butcher and a neighborhood deli. HOW TO SHOP · WHAT TO ASK YOUR BUTCHER
"What ranch did this come from?"
A real butcher knows. If they don't, that's information.
"Is this dry-aged or wet-aged?"
Dry-aged loses moisture and concentrates flavor. Wet-aged (plastic bag) is cheaper and more common.
"Do you have any secondary cuts?"
Whole-animal butchers have bavette, spider steak, teres major — things supermarkets never carry.
"Can I get the bones?"
Any butcher who buys whole animals has bones. Often free or very cheap. The best stock you'll ever make.
