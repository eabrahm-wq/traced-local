↑ TRACED LOCAL · SAN FRANCISCO

Specialty
Goods

Producers who do one thing and do it completely. Olive oil pressed from a single grove. Chocolate made from beans sourced from one farm. Bread from a single milling tradition. Honey from hives with a known address.

Single-origin, single-ingredient, fully traceable.

THE POINT

Most specialty food is marketed as special but made at commodity scale. "Artisan" bread from a 50,000-loaf-per-day facility. "Cold-pressed" olive oil blended from five countries. "Small-batch" chocolate that isn't.

The producers in this guide make something specific, from a specific source, in limited quantity — and they can tell you exactly what it is.

BREAD — SLOW FERMENTATION, LOCAL GRAIN
Tartine Bakery
Country loaves & morning pastries · Mission · Est. 2002
SLOW FERMENT▼
Chad Robertson's country loaf — thick dark crust, open crumb, 4-hour window to buy on weekdays — defined a generation of American bread baking. Tartine uses slow-fermented levain, stone-milled heritage grain flours, and a wood-fired oven. The Manufactory on Alabama Street expanded the operation significantly, but the Guerrero Street original remains the most coveted loaf in SF.
WHAT THEY MAKE
Country loaf (levain)Whole wheat loafMorning bunsCroissantsSeasonal pastries
Address: 600 Guerrero St, Mission (original) · 595 Alabama St (Manufactory)
Loaves: Weekdays ~5 PM, Sat/Sun ~8 AM (sell out fast)
Josey Baker Bread
Heritage grain, stone-milled · The Mill, Western Addition
HERITAGE GRAIN▼
Josey Baker mills his own flour from heritage grains — emmer, einkorn, rye — at The Mill on Divisadero, a collaboration with Four Barrel Coffee (now independent). He is one of the few American bakers who controls the grain-to-loaf chain entirely. His bread has more flavor and nutrition than anything made from commodity white flour, and his approach to whole-grain baking has influenced bakers across the country.
WHAT THEY MAKE
Heritage whole grain loavesEmmer breadRye breadMilled grain for sale
Address: 736 Divisadero St, Western Addition (The Mill)
Also: Sells freshly milled flour by the bag
Marla Bakery
Wood-fired breads & pastries · Inner Richmond · Exclusive at Clement St Market
MARKET EXCLUSIVE▼
Marla Bakery holds an exclusive vendor spot at the Clement Street Farmers Market — one of only three bakeries with that designation. Their breads are wood-fired with long fermentation, and their pastry work leans savory: galettes, hand pies, and seasonal preparations that use whatever the surrounding produce vendors are selling. The combination of their Inner Richmond retail presence and Clement Street exclusivity makes them a neighborhood institution.
WHAT THEY MAKE
Wood-fired country loavesPastriesSavory galettesSeasonal preparations
Retail: 3619 Balboa St, Inner Richmond
Market: Clement Street Farmers Market (Sun) — exclusive
Daymoon Bread
Naturally leavened · Clement Street Market exclusive
MARKET EXCLUSIVE▼
Daymoon holds the third exclusive bakery spot at Clement Street, alongside Marla and Saltwater. Naturally leavened loaves with extended fermentation — the approach prioritizes flavor development and digestibility over speed. Their presence at Clement Street is Sunday-only and quantities are limited.
WHAT THEY MAKE
Naturally leavened loavesSourdough varietiesSeasonal pastries
Market: Clement Street Farmers Market (Sun) — exclusive
CHOCOLATE — BEAN-TO-BAR, SINGLE ORIGIN
Dandelion Chocolate
Bean-to-bar · Mission · Founded 2010
BEAN-TO-BAR▼
Dandelion is one of the original American bean-to-bar chocolate makers and among the best in the world at what they do. Each bar is made from a single origin — meaning cacao from one farm or cooperative in one country, processed entirely in-house on Valencia Street. Two ingredients only: cacao and sugar. Every bar has a harvest date and a farm name. The tasting room on Valencia is worth visiting to understand what single-origin chocolate can actually taste like compared to commodity chocolate.
WHAT THEY MAKE
Single-origin dark chocolate barsDrinking chocolateBonbons (seasonal)Baked goods
Address: 740 Valencia St, Mission
Also: Ferry Building retail shop
TCHO Chocolate
Mission-based manufacturer · Direct farmer partnerships
▼
TCHO pioneered direct relationships with cacao farmers and a flavor profiling system that helps farmers understand how their beans translate to finished chocolate — creating a feedback loop that improves quality at the source. They work with cooperatives in Ghana, Peru, Ecuador, and Madagascar. Their approach is less artisan-romantic than Dandelion but more systematically committed to traceable farmer relationships.
WHAT THEY MAKE
Direct-trade dark chocolateMilk chocolateBaking chocolateChocolate chips
Available: Bi-Rite, Rainbow Grocery, Ferry Building shops
OLIVE OIL — CALIFORNIA SINGLE-ESTATE
California Olive Ranch
Single-estate · Artisan tier · Oroville, CA
CALIFORNIA GROWN▼
California Olive Ranch's artisan tier (distinct from their mass-market bottles) is cold-pressed from California-grown olives within hours of harvest. California olive oil has a distinct profile — grassy, peppery, high polyphenol content — compared to the mellow Italian or Spanish oils most Americans know. Fresh California oil, harvested in fall and pressed immediately, is one of the most underappreciated pantry items available in the state.
WHAT THEY PRODUCE
Single-estate EVOOArbequina varietalArbosana varietalKoroneiki varietal
Available: Bi-Rite, Gus's, Rainbow Grocery, specialty shops
Note: Look for harvest date on label — buy within 18 months
McEvoy Ranch
Estate olive oil · Petaluma, Marin County
MARIN COUNTY▼
McEvoy Ranch in Petaluma produces certified organic estate olive oil from Italian varietals grown in Marin County's unique climate. The ranch is one of the pioneers of California artisan olive oil and sells at the Ferry Plaza Farmers Market and in the Ferry Building. Marin's fog and temperature conditions produce oil with a distinctive quality — bright, fresh, with pronounced grassiness.
WHAT THEY PRODUCE
Certified organic EVOOEstate blendsFlavored oils (seasonal)
At: Ferry Plaza Farmers Market (Sat) · Ferry Building retail
Ranch: Petaluma, Marin County
CHEESE — FARMSTEAD & ARTISAN, BAY AREA
Cowgirl Creamery
Certified organic, Point Reyes · Ferry Building & Pt Reyes Station
CERTIFIED ORGANIC▼
Sue Conley and Peggy Smith founded Cowgirl Creamery in Point Reyes Station in 1997, sourcing certified organic milk from Strauss Family Creamery. Their Mt. Tam — a triple-cream soft-ripened cheese — is one of the defining cheeses of the American artisan movement. The Ferry Building shop in SF lets city residents access the full range without driving to West Marin. Cowgirl also appears at the Ferry Plaza Saturday market.
WHAT THEY MAKE
Mt. Tam (triple cream)Red Hawk (washed rind)Cottage cheeseCrème fraîcheSeasonal varieties
Address: Ferry Building, Shop 17
Original creamery: Point Reyes Station, Marin
Achadinha Cheese Company
Farmstead goat cheese · Petaluma · at Divisadero Market
FARMSTEAD▼
The Pacheco family makes farmstead goat cheese in Petaluma — milk from their own animals, cheese made on-site. Their aged Capricious and fresh chèvre appear at several Bay Area farmers markets including the Divisadero Sunday market. Farmstead means the milk never leaves the farm before it becomes cheese, producing a more direct expression of the animals' diet and the specific landscape.
WHAT THEY MAKE
Fresh chèvreCapricious (aged)Seasonal flavored varieties
At: Divisadero Farmers Market (Sun) · Multiple Bay Area markets
Farm: Petaluma, Sonoma County
HONEY — SINGLE-SOURCE, BAY AREA HIVES
Alex's Family Bee Farm
Raw varietal honey · Fillmore Farmers Market
RAW HONEY▼
Alex's Family Bee Farm keeps hives in multiple Northern California locations and produces raw varietal honey — meaning each jar reflects what was blooming in a specific location during a specific season. Wildflower honey from hives near the coast tastes completely different from sage honey from inland hives. They appear regularly at the Fillmore Farmers Market, bringing multiple varietal options to the Western Addition every Saturday.
WHAT THEY PRODUCE
Raw wildflower honeyVarietal single-source honeysSeasonal varieties
At: Fillmore Farmers Market (Sat 9 AM–1 PM)
MUSHROOMS — CULTIVATED & FORAGED, COASTAL CALIFORNIA
Far West Fungi
Specialty & medicinal mushrooms · Moss Landing · 6 SF markets
6 MARKETS▼
The Garrone family has been cultivating specialty mushrooms in Moss Landing, Monterey County since the late 1980s. They appear at six SF farmers markets weekly — Ferry Plaza, Alemany, Castro, Mission Community, and others — making them the most consistently available specialty mushroom source in the city. Their range spans everyday shiitake and oyster mushrooms through lion's mane, maitake, and medicinal reishi and turkey tail.
WHAT THEY GROW
Lion's ManeMaitakeShiitakeKing TrumpetOyster (multiple colors)ReishiTurkey TailDried mushrooms
At: Ferry Plaza (Sat/Tue/Thu) · Alemany (Sat) · Castro (Wed) · Mission Community (Thu)
Farm: Moss Landing, Monterey County
